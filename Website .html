Problem With Today's Clothing

The problem with today's sports equipment is that you still sweat in it and it doesn't protect you from the freezing temperatures in the winter.

Heat Strokes

The Centers for Disease Control (CDC) published a study in 2010 that stated heat illness is the leading cause of death and disability among high school athletes. The CDC estimated that it's responsible for an average of more than 9,000 heat illnesses among high school athletes annually. Annual Survey of Football Injury Research, 1931-2009. The number of heat-related injuries from 1997 to 2006 increased 133 percent.

Sweating

It’s a nuisance, but surely you know that. Moisture on the skin seeps into clothing, making one’s sweat obvious, which is generally considered unattractive, and creates a perfect medium for bacterial growth and the odor that ensues. Wet skin is softer and more vulnerable to abrasion (blisters) or other forms of physical damage.Once the sweat evaporates, salt is left behind and can become quite uncomfortable when the skin finally dries. Salt from sweat will leave salt line stains on gloves, boots and other clothing.
